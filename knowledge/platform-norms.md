# Platform Norms & Research Approach

---

## Instagram

### How the Algorithm Works for Research
- Hashtag search surfaces recent and top posts — not all accounts use every hashtag
- Top posts in a hashtag = highest engagement accounts in that niche — prioritize these
- "Similar accounts" feature on profiles is a goldmine — Instagram clusters similar content creators
- Who comments on a high-value account = more high-value accounts to check

### Bio Patterns to Recognize
- Medical mom bios: "Mom to [name] 💙 [diagnosis] warrior | Sharing our journey"
- BCBA/therapist bios: "BCBA 🧠 | Helping families navigate autism | DM for info"
- Course sellers: "✨ Grab my free guide 👇 | [linktr.ee/...]"
- Foundations: "Supporting [diagnosis] families nationwide | [website]"

### USA Signal Detection
- State abbreviations in bio (TX, CA, FL, NY, etc.)
- City mentions
- #IEP hashtag (US-specific education system)
- #Medicaid or #SSI mentions
- References to specific US children's hospitals (CHOP, Boston Children's, etc.)
- USD pricing in products/courses
- US school system references ("back to school", "504 plan")

### Email Extraction from Instagram
Order of operations:
1. Check bio directly — some accounts display email@domain.com or use "email me at..."
2. Check link in bio — click through to website
3. On website: check /contact, /about, /work-with-me pages
4. Look for mailto: links anywhere on the site
5. Check Linktree (if bio links to one) — sometimes has direct email link
6. Hunter.io — only after configuring API key

### Engagement Quality Check
- Pull last 3 posts
- Count comments that are actual sentences (not emoji-only, not "so beautiful")
- Look for back-and-forth — does the account owner reply? That signals a real community.
- Check if the same 5 accounts comment every post (small circle) vs. diverse commenters (real reach)

## Facebook Groups

### Why Facebook is Different
- Groups are private — you cannot scrape members or posts
- What you CAN find: group name, member count, group description, admin names
- Active groups show recent post timestamps in the group preview
- Admin contact info is often available through their personal profile link

### How to Find USA Groups
Search queries that work well:
- "[diagnosis] moms USA"
- "medical moms [US state]"
- "autism parents [city/state]"
- "Down syndrome families America"
- "[diagnosis] support group USA"

Also: check the "groups" tab on high-value individual Facebook profiles — people in these communities are usually in multiple relevant groups.

### Admin Contact Approach
- Admin names are listed in group "About" section
- Click admin name → view profile → look for email, website, or Messenger contact
- Some admins list email or business page in their personal profile
- Note: "Admin" in the sheet means the name of the group admin, not necessarily an email address

### Group Activity Signals
- "Most recent activity" shown in group preview — check for posts within 7 days
- Groups with 5,000+ members but no recent activity are dead — skip
- Pinned posts with recent dates = active moderation = active group

## Reddit

### How Reddit Works for Research
- Subreddits are public — all data accessible via API
- Moderators are listed in the sidebar of every subreddit
- Subscriber count and creation date visible
- Post frequency and top posts visible without login

### What Makes a Reddit Community High-Value
- Active moderation (mods who respond, enforce rules)
- Daily new posts — not just weekly
- Long comment threads (not just upvotes)
- Mod-pinned resources or wiki = organized, serious community
- Community has specific rules (shows active management)

### How to Find Mod Contact Info
- Mod usernames listed in subreddit sidebar
- Can message via Reddit (u/modname) — note username in Admin column
- Some mods list email in profile bio
- Mod team can be messaged via r/subredditname/about/moderators

### Reddit Search Strategy
Start with known subreddits, then:
- Look at "related subreddits" listed in sidebars
- Search Reddit directly: site:reddit.com "[diagnosis] parents"
- Check what subreddits high-value post authors belong to
- r/findareddit can help surface niche communities

## General Cross-Platform Principles

**Think in clusters, not single accounts.**
Every account you find is a door to 5 more. Who do they follow? Who comments on them? What hashtags do they use that you haven't seen? This is how the list grows from 50 to 5,000 quality entries.

**Recency matters.**
A 200K-follower account with their last post from 8 months ago is worthless. A 15K-follower account posting daily with 40 comments per post is gold.

**Trust signals over vanity metrics.**
Followers can be bought. Comments that say "We went through the exact same thing" cannot. Prioritize real conversations over large numbers.
