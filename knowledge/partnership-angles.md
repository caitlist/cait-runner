# Partnership Angles by Account Type

These are the "why would they say yes" frameworks for each list type.
Use these when generating Notes in the sheet and when drafting outreach (if requested).

---

## BCBAs with Online Products

**Their situation:** They have a course, membership, or coaching program for parents of autistic children. Their income depends on parents trusting and implementing what they teach.

**CAIT angle:** CAIT is the tool parents use to apply what they learned. Their students can use CAIT to track their child's ABA progress, log behavioral data between sessions, and generate summaries to share with the BCBA. CAIT doesn't compete with the BCBA — it makes their clients more organized, more compliant, and more engaged.

**Mutual benefit framing:** "Recommend CAIT to your community and your students will show up to sessions better prepared. That makes your program look more effective."

**Notes field to add:** Product name if found, course topic, rough audience size.

---

## OTs and SLPs Selling Products

**Their situation:** Running a small business (private practice + online content). Constantly creating content about home therapy activities, sensory strategies, AAC, feeding therapy.

**CAIT angle:** Parents need a place to track therapy progress between sessions. CAIT captures what they practiced at home, generates summaries for therapy sessions, and reduces the "I forgot what we worked on last week" problem. OTs and SLPs get more informed parents in every session.

**Mutual benefit framing:** "Your parents will be more prepared for sessions and your home program compliance goes up."

---

## Pediatric Doctors and Nurse Educators

**Their situation:** High trust, large medical-parent audiences on Instagram and TikTok. Often sell parent education courses or books.

**CAIT angle:** CAIT generates clinician-ready PDF summaries. This is directly valuable — parents show up to appointments organized instead of describing symptoms from memory. The doctor's time is used better.

**Mutual benefit framing:** "CAIT is the tool you wish all your patients' families had — recommend it and they'll show up to appointments prepared."

---

## Educators and IEP Course Sellers

**Their situation:** Selling courses to parents navigating special education — IEP prep, 504 plans, advocacy training.

**CAIT angle:** Parents learning to advocate for their child need a place to track their child's progress, organize documentation, and generate summaries for IEP meetings. CAIT is the organizational backbone for everything the course teaches.

**Mutual benefit framing:** "Your students need a tool to implement what they learn — CAIT is where their IEP notes, progress tracking, and documentation live."

---

## Journey Accounts (50 Million List)

**Their situation:** They ARE the user. They document their child's medical journey daily. Their followers are CAIT's exact target user.

**CAIT angle:** These accounts don't need a "why would CAIT benefit them" — they would benefit from CAIT themselves. The outreach approach is: introduce them to CAIT as a tool for their own family first, then ask if they'd share it with their community.

**Framing:** This is not a transactional partnership. This is showing a kindred spirit a tool that would make their life easier, and trusting that if it genuinely helps them, they'll share it organically.

**What to note in the sheet:** What diagnosis they cover, follower count tier, engagement quality score, whether they appear to be USA-based.

---

## Facebook Group Admins

**Their situation:** Running a community for free, often for years. They feel responsible for protecting their group from spam and genuinely care about what they share.

**CAIT angle:** Admins want to provide value to their group. CAIT is a free tool (at launch) that their members would find genuinely useful — not a product pitch. Position it as a resource announcement, not an ad.

**Framing:** "We'd love to share a free resource with your community — not a pitch, a tool."

**What to note in the sheet:** Group size, diagnosis focus, admin name, whether the group appears to allow resource sharing.

---

## Foundations and Advocacy Organizations

**Their situation:** Their entire mission is to serve families with the relevant diagnosis. They have email lists, social followings, and community trust that took years to build.

**CAIT angle:** CAIT serves the exact families they exist for. A partnership doesn't have to be complex — a shared post, an email newsletter mention, or a co-announcement at launch can give CAIT immediate trust by association.

**The right contact:** Executive Director, Director of Programs, or Director of Communications. Not the general inbox.

**Framing:** "We want to make CAIT available to the families you serve — can we partner on an awareness push at launch?"

**Notes to collect:** Organization name, website, mission statement, relevant diagnosis, estimated community size, executive contact if findable.
