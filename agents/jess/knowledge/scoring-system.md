# Scoring System — How Jess Evaluates Influencers

---

## The Two Scores

Every account gets scored on two dimensions. Both go on the sheet. Both matter.

### 1. ER Score (Engagement Rate — Tiered)

ER% = (avg likes + avg comments) / followers × 100

Benchmarked against follower tier:

| Tier | Followers | Low Floor | High Floor | What it means |
|------|-----------|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Macro | 500K–5M | 0.8% | 2.0% | Massive reach, lower engagement normal |
| Mid-tier | 100K–500K | 1.5% | 3.5% | Sweet spot for CAIT |
| Micro | 10K–100K | 3.0% | 6.0% | High engagement, smaller reach |
| Nano | <10K | 5.0% | 10.0% | Very high engagement, niche |

Score: **High** (above high floor) / **Good** (between floors) / **Low** (below low floor)

### 2. Comment Score (Raw Volume)

Raw average comments per post. No benchmarking — just the number.

This is the boss's primary metric. A mid-tier account with 200 avg comments beats a macro with 50 avg comments every time for CAIT's purposes.

Score buckets:
- **Strong:** 50+ avg comments
- **Decent:** 20–50 avg comments
- **Weak:** under 20 avg comments

---

## Follower Range

Target: **40K – 250K**

- Under 40K: reach too small for launch goals (still consider if comment count is exceptional)
- Over 250K: harder to partner with, less authentic community feel
- Over 500K: macro territory — flag for Cherwin, don't auto-approve

---

## Comment Pod Detection

Flag immediately if: **avg comments > 2x avg likes**

Real audiences like more than they comment. If comments dwarf likes, it's a pod — a group of accounts that trade comments to artificially inflate numbers. These accounts look great on paper and perform like zero in practice.

Flag as "Comment Pod — verify" in Notes column. Do not approve without Cherwin sign-off.

---

## Content Disqualifiers

Auto-flag (do not approve without discussion):
- Amazon storefront link in bio or posts
- "Use code [X] for discount" in recent captions
- Affiliate hashtags (#ad, #sponsored, #gifted on most posts — occasional is fine)
- Product-first content (reviewing products every post)
- Loop giveaway posts in recent history

These accounts have audiences built on product interest, not community trust. CAIT needs the latter.

---

## Strong Account Profile

What a strong account looks like:
- 40K–250K followers
- 50+ avg comments per post
- ER% in the "Good" or "High" range for their tier
- Comments read like real parents sharing their own stories
- Content is day-in-the-life, honest, not product-heavy
- USA signals in bio or content
- Posted within last 30 days

---

## Loop Giveaway Flag

If an account has participated in loop giveaways (posts where 10+ accounts all promote the same prize, requiring follows to enter) — their follower count and ER are inflated. These followers entered for a prize, not for the content. Flag in Loop Giveaway History column.
