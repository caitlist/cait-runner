## What This Skill Does

Drafts personalized outreach messages (email or DM) for qualified accounts in the sheet. Every message is tailored to the specific account's content, product, and audience. No templates. No em dashes. No "I hope this finds you well."

## When to Use It

ONLY when Cherwin explicitly asks. Examples:
- "draft me an email for @[username]"
- "write a DM for [foundation name]"
- "can you write outreach for the BCBAs in the sheet?"

Do NOT use when simply adding accounts to the sheet.

## Steps

1. **Read the sheet entry** — Pull: username, category, product/course, email, notes, tier
2. **Read the account bio** (if accessible) — get their voice and audience
3. **Identify the angle** — from `knowledge/partnership-angles.md`
4. **Draft the message** — match format to channel:
   - **Email:** Subject line + 3-paragraph body (warm opener, value prop, specific ask)
   - **Instagram DM:** 3–5 sentences max, very casual, no pitch energy
   - **Facebook DM to group admin:** 2–3 sentences, community-first framing

## Voice Rules (Non-Negotiable)

- No em dashes — ever
- No corporate language ("synergies", "leverage", "partnership opportunity")
- No "I hope this message finds you well"
- No "I came across your account and was blown away"
- Sound like a human who actually uses the product and knows their work
- Subject line for emails: direct and specific, not clickbait
- Always reference something specific from their bio or content — never generic

## Output Format

```
Channel: Email / Instagram DM / Facebook DM
Subject: [if email]

---
[Message body]
---

Notes: [Any personalization flags or alternatives Cherwin might want]
```

## Example Tone

Bad: "I hope this email finds you well! I came across your amazing account and I think there's a real synergy between your brand and ours."

Good: "Hey Sarah, I've been following your ABA parent training course and noticed your students often ask about tracking their kids' progress between sessions. I run growth for a new app called CAIT that's built exactly for that — would love to share it with your community if you think it'd be useful."
